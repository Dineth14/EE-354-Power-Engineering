Key Equations of Power Engineering

Click to reveal the physical meaning

$$P_i = \sum_{k=1}^{n} |V_i||V_k|\big(G_{ik}\cos\theta_{ik} + B_{ik}\sin\theta_{ik}\big)$$
Power Flow Equation: The real power injected at bus $i$ equals the sum of power transfers to every other bus $k$, determined by bus voltage magnitudes, the conductance $G_{ik}$ and susceptance $B_{ik}$ of the admittance matrix, and the voltage angle difference $\theta_{ik} = \delta_i - \delta_k$.
$$M\frac{d^2\delta}{dt^2} = P_m - P_e = P_m - \frac{E_f V_t}{X_s}\sin\delta$$
Swing Equation: The rotor's angular acceleration is proportional to the difference between mechanical input power $P_m$ and electrical output power $P_e$. The inertia constant $M = 2H/\omega_s$ determines how quickly the rotor responds. This is the foundation of transient stability analysis.
$$T = \frac{3}{\omega_s} \cdot \frac{V_1^2 \, R_2'/s}{\left(R_1 + R_2'/s\right)^2 + \left(X_1 + X_2'\right)^2}$$
Induction Motor Torque: Developed torque depends on slip $s$, rotor resistance $R_2'$, and the Thevenin impedance seen from the rotor. Maximum (breakdown) torque occurs at a specific slip $s_T$ and is independent of rotor resistance — only the slip at which it occurs changes.
$$Z_{pu,new} = Z_{pu,old} \times \frac{kV_{old}^2}{kV_{new}^2} \times \frac{MVA_{new}}{MVA_{old}}$$
Per-Unit Base Conversion: When converting impedances between per-unit systems with different voltage or power bases, this formula scales the per-unit value. The per-unit system simplifies power system calculations by normalising quantities to common bases, eliminating transformer turns ratios.
