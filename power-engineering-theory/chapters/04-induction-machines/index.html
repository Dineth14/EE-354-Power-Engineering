Overview

The three-phase induction motor is the most widely used electric machine in industry. Its rugged construction and self-starting capability make it the default choice for most motor applications. Understanding its equivalent circuit, torque production, and speed control methods is essential for power engineers.

Per-Phase Equivalent Circuit

The IEEE-recommended per-phase equivalent circuit (referred to the stator):

1

Stator Branch

Series connection of stator resistance $R_1$ and leakage reactance $X_1$.

$$Z_1 = R_1 + jX_1$$
2

Magnetising Branch

Shunt branch representing core losses ($R_c$) and magnetising reactance ($X_m$).

$$Z_m = jX_m \quad \text{(simplified, neglecting } R_c\text{)}$$
3

Rotor Branch (Referred to Stator)

The slip-dependent rotor impedance:

$$Z_2 = \frac{R_2}{s} + jX_2 = R_2 + jX_2 + R_2\,\frac{1-s}{s}$$

where $R_2/s$ is the total rotor impedance and $R_2(1-s)/s$ represents mechanical power conversion.

Slip & Speed Relations

$$n_s = \frac{120 f}{p} \quad \text{(synchronous speed, rpm)}$$
$$s = \frac{n_s - n_r}{n_s} = \frac{\omega_s - \omega_r}{\omega_s}$$

where $p$ is the number of poles, $f$ is the supply frequency, and $n_r$ is the rotor speed.

Torque Equation

1

Air-Gap Power

Power transferred across the air gap (per phase):

$$P_{ag} = I_2^2 \cdot \frac{R_2}{s}$$
2

Electromagnetic Torque

$$T_e = \frac{3 P_{ag}}{\omega_s} = \frac{3}{\omega_s} \cdot \frac{V_{th}^2 (R_2/s)}{(R_{th} + R_2/s)^2 + (X_{th} + X_2)^2}$$
3

Maximum (Pull-Out) Torque

Setting $dT/ds = 0$:

$$T_{max} = \frac{3V_{th}^2}{2\omega_s\left(R_{th} + \sqrt{R_{th}^2 + (X_{th}+X_2)^2}\right)}$$

at slip:

$$s_{max} = \frac{R_2}{\sqrt{R_{th}^2 + (X_{th}+X_2)^2}}$$

Note: $T_{max}$ is independent of $R_2$ — changing rotor resistance only shifts the slip at which maximum torque occurs.

Torque-Speed Characteristic

The T-n curve has three distinct regions:

  • Low-slip region ($s \ll s_{max}$): torque approximately proportional to slip — near-linear behaviour
  • Pull-out point ($s = s_{max}$): maximum torque — safe operating limit
  • High-slip region ($s > s_{max}$): torque decreases — unstable operation, motor stalls

Worked Example — Motor Operating Point

A 3-phase, 4-pole, 50 Hz induction motor has $R_2 = 0.02$ pu and $X_1 + X_2 = 0.12$ pu. Find the slip at maximum torque.

$$s_{max} = \frac{R_2}{\sqrt{R_1^2 + (X_1 + X_2)^2}} \approx \frac{0.02}{0.12} = 0.167$$

Speed at max torque: $n_r = n_s(1 - s_{max}) = 1500(1 - 0.167) = 1250$ rpm

Effect of Rotor Resistance

Increasing external rotor resistance (wound-rotor machines):

  • Shifts $T_{max}$ to higher slip (lower speed)
  • Does not change the magnitude of $T_{max}$
  • Increases starting torque (up to $T_{max}$ when $s_{max} = 1$)
  • Reduces efficiency at rated speed

The condition for maximum starting torque: $R_2 + R_{ext} = \sqrt{R_1^2 + (X_1+X_2)^2}$

Speed Control — V/f and VFD

1

Constant V/f Principle

Maintaining $V/f = \text{constant}$ keeps the air-gap flux constant, preserving torque capability:

$$\Phi \propto \frac{V}{f} = \text{constant}$$
2

Below Base Speed

Constant torque region: reduce both $V$ and $f$ proportionally. $n_s = 120f/p$ decreases, but full torque is available.

3

Above Base Speed (Field Weakening)

$V$ cannot exceed rated; increase $f$ alone. Flux decreases, giving constant power operation:

$$T \propto \frac{1}{f^2}, \quad P \propto \frac{1}{f}$$

Slip-Power Recovery (Doubly-Fed)

In wound-rotor machines, the slip power $sP_{ag}$ normally dissipated in rotor resistance can be recovered and fed back to the supply through a converter, significantly improving efficiency at reduced speeds.

Induction Generator

When driven above synchronous speed ($s < 0$), the induction machine generates power. The torque becomes negative (braking mechanical input).

  • Requires reactive power from the grid (or capacitor bank) for excitation
  • Self-excited induction generator (SEIG): uses capacitors across terminals
  • Common in wind turbines (Type 1 & Type 2 wind generators)
$$P_{gen} = P_{ag}(1 - s) = P_{ag}|s| \cdot \frac{1-s}{|s|} \quad (s < 0)$$

$P_{ag} = 3I_2^2 \dfrac{R_2}{s}$

$P_{mech} = (1-s)\, P_{ag}$
